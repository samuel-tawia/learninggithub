Brief History of Electric Power

Thomas Edison's first electric power plant generated 110-volt direct current (DC) for lighting and a variable machinery in a nearby buildings.
But efficient transmission of power over long distances requires higher voltages.
In those days,the only way to transform DC volttage was to use a "genset"- a Dc motor driving a DC generator.That's two rotating machines,with four windings.

Then Nikola Tesla invented the alternating current (AC) induction motor and transformer.With constant AC frequency,an induction motors speed cannot vary,but it is cheaper and more durable than a DC geneset. so at the dawn of the 20th century,AC won "the war of currents."

But not forever.In the middle of 20th century,computers began consuming progressively more DC power.Then came the insulated-gate bipolar transformer (IGBT) and the voltage-source converter (VSC).A VSC can make variable-frequency AC which runs induction motors at variable speed and it easily transform DC

DC power,computer control, and the internet are helping the power grid accept variable renewable resources like wind and solar.And they are improving electrocsl distribution systems on campuses,in neighborhoods and in buildings.With local energy generation and storage,distribution systems are evolving into robust "microgrids."

*1882: Edison's Pearl Street DC power plant in New York
*1884: Tesla invents closed-core AC transformer in Budapest
*1886: Thorenberg AC power plant in Lucerne
*1985: insulated-gate bipolar transistor by Toshiba
*2012: UCSD megawatt microgrid in southern california
